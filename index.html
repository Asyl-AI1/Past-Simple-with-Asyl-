‚è∞ Time Machine Grammar

The Past Simple is for actions that are finished. It's like a time machine that takes you to the past to tell a story about what happened!

Usage & Time Expressions

We use the Past Simple for actions that started and finished in the past. We often use it with specific time words.

  • Finished actions: I watched a movie yesterday.
  • Series of finished actions: I finished my homework, went to the park, and met my friends.
  • Time expressions: yesterday, last week, last month, last year, a week ago, in 2020, this morning.

The Formula

Type Formula Example
Positive (+) I + verb + -ed I listened to music.
Negative (-) I + did not + verb I didn't listen to music.
Question (?) Did + you + verb? Did you listen to music?
